The AI for Good Young Leaders Hub (Lagos) is a youth-led, community-centred platform that brings artificial intelligence to the people. We begin by listening because community voice guides every stage of our activities from problem discovery through design, piloting, and deployment. Our hub connects university student chapters, early-career practitioners, mentors, and local partners to co-design solutions, inform policy, and build capacity that responds to locally defined needs.

Our Core Activities

Beyond building prototypes - comprehensive impact

Our work is explicitly aligned to the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and extends far beyond building prototypes. We run capacity-building programs, conduct applied research, convene policy dialogues, incubate promising ideas, and mobilize partnerships for sustainable scale.

Our Mission

Empowering communities through ethical AI innovation

Community-Centered AI

We believe AI should be developed with communities, not for them. Our mission is to co-create ethical AI solutions that amplify local voices, respect cultural contexts, and deliver measurable social impact aligned to the UN Sustainable Development Goals.

Every project begins with community engagement, ensuring that technology serves humanity and addresses locally-defined priorities through participatory design processes.

Youth Leadership

As part of the global AI for Good network, we empower young leaders to drive ethical AI innovation across Lagos and West Africa. We provide mentorship, resources, and platforms for emerging practitioners to develop technical skills while building sustainable local expertise.

Our hub connects university student chapters, early-career professionals, and community partners to create lasting change through collaborative leadership and knowledge sharing.

Our Approach

Systematic methodology for community-driven AI development

1

Community Engagement

We start by listening. Through Community AI Labs and participatory workshops, we work directly with local communities to understand challenges, priorities, and cultural contexts that should guide AI development.

2

Co-Design Process

Technical teams collaborate with community members to design solutions that reflect lived experience. We ensure ethical compliance, cultural sensitivity, and alignment with UN SDGs throughout the development process.

3

Pilot & Validate

Solutions are tested in real-world contexts with continuous community feedback. We measure impact against relevant SDGs and adjust based on outcomes and community input.

4

Scale & Sustain

Successful pilots are scaled through partnerships with local organizations, government agencies, and academic institutions. We build local capacity to ensure long-term sustainability and community ownership.
